# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

> **Prepared as part of CSE0001 Digital Literacy Project | VIT Bhopal University**

Maintaining a responsible digital footprint is essential for academic success and future career prospects. Below is a guide on how to navigate the digital world effectively.

---

## ✅ The DO's

* **Keep your profiles professional:** Use a clear profile photo, your real name, and a bio that reflects your academic and career interests. Employers and internship coordinators often check social media.
* **Use privacy settings wisely:** Regularly review settings on Instagram, Facebook, and Snapchat. Limit personal data (family photos, location, phone number) to trusted connections only.
* **Think before you post:** Ask yourself: *"Would I be comfortable if my professor or future employer saw this?"* If the answer is no, do not post it.
* **Share knowledge and useful content:** Post articles, academic achievements, project updates, and event participation. This builds a credible online identity.
* **Engage respectfully with others:** Comment constructively and maintain a polite tone, even when you disagree with someone’s opinion.
* **Cite sources when sharing information:** If you share a statistic or news story, mention the source to maintain credibility and avoid spreading misinformation.
* **Report harmful or abusive content:** Use platform reporting tools to flag cyberbullying or fake news. Do not engage with trolls or retaliate.

---

## ❌ The DON'Ts

* **Don't share personal or financial information:** Never post your phone number, Aadhaar number, UPI PIN, bank details, or OTPs publicly or in DMs.
* **Don't post or share unverified news:** Forwarding fake news damages your reputation. Verify information on fact-checking sites like *Fact Crescendo* or *Alt News* before sharing.
* **Don't engage in online arguments:** Heated exchanges and trolling leave a permanent digital footprint that is difficult to erase and can be used against you later.
* **Don't accept requests from strangers:** Fake accounts are common. Always vet a profile carefully before connecting with unknown individuals.
* **Don't post content that could embarrass you:** Party photos, offensive jokes, or controversial rants may affect admissions, scholarships, and job offers in the future.
* **Don't ignore cyberbullying:** If you or others are targeted, take screenshots as evidence, block the user, and report the incident at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in).

## Summary Comparison

| Feature | Action to Take | Action to Avoid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Identity** | Professional Bio & Real Name | Offensive Usernames & Fake IDs |

| **Security** | Reporting Abusive Content | Sharing OTPs or UPI PINs |
| **Content** | Educational & Achievement Based | Unverified News & "Party" Photos |
| **Interaction** | Constructive & Polite | Trolling & Heated Arguments |
