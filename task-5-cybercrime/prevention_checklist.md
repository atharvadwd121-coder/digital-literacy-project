# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

> **Prepared as part of CSE0001 Digital Literacy Project | VIT Bhopal University**

Awareness is the first line of defense against cybercrime. Follow this comprehensive guide to secure your digital presence and financial assets.

---

## 🔐 Account & Password Security

* **Create Strong, Unique Passwords:** Use at least **12 characters** with a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using the same password for multiple sites.
* **Use a Password Manager:** If you struggle to remember complex passwords, use a reputable password manager to store them securely.
* **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Turn on 2FA for all critical accounts, including email, social media, and especially banking/UPI platforms. This adds a vital second layer of verification.

---

## 🌐 Web & Device Safety

* **Beware of Suspicious Links:** Never click on links in unsolicited emails or SMS, even if they appear to come from someone you know. These are often "phishing" attempts to steal your credentials.
* **Verify Authorities:** If you receive a call from someone claiming to be a bank or government official, **hang up**. Call the official number found on the organization's verified website to verify the claim.
* **Update Regularly:** Keep your operating system and apps updated. These updates often contain "security patches" that fix vulnerabilities exploited by hackers.
* **Use Official App Stores:** Only download banking or UPI applications from the **Google Play Store** or **Apple App Store**. Avoid "sideloading" apps from unverified websites.

---

## 💸 Financial & UPI Safety (Critical)

* **The PIN Secret:** Never share your **UPI PIN** or **OTP** with anyone—not even bank officials, friends, or family.
* **QR Code Rule:** Scanning a QR code is for **sending money**, not receiving it. If someone asks you to scan a code to "receive a refund," it is a scam.
* **Verify Payee Identity:** Always double-check the actual name of the recipient before hitting 'Pay.' Do not rely solely on profile pictures or phone numbers.
* **Set Transaction Limits:** Use your banking app to set a daily limit on UPI transactions to minimize potential damage in case of a breach.
* **Avoid Public Wi-Fi:** Never perform banking or UPI transactions on public/open Wi-Fi networks. These are insecure and easily monitored by attackers.

---

## 🚨 Emergency Reporting Channels

If you or someone you know falls victim to cybercrime, act immediately. Time is the most critical factor in recovering stolen funds.

| Channel | Contact Details | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in) | File an official online complaint. |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** | Call 24/7 for immediate financial fraud reporting. |
| **Local Police** | Nearest Police Station | File an FIR under the provisions of the IT Act. |

**Important:** If you are a victim of UPI fraud, call **1930** immediately. The sooner the report is filed, the higher the chance of the bank freezing the stolen funds before the fraudster withdraws them.
